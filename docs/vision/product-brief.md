# Product Brief / Vision

> The north star. Everything else (requirements, architecture, roadmap) should trace
> back to this. Keep it short and opinionated. **DRAFT — to be filled in together.**

## One-line pitch

_A self-hosted home media server that just works on the devices we already own._

## The problem

_What's wrong with the status quo (Plex/Jellyfin/Emby, or no solution)? Why build
rather than adopt? (Learning, control, cost, privacy, specific feature gaps...)_

## Who it's for (primary personas)

See [personas](../requirements/personas.md).

## What success looks like

_Concrete, observable outcomes. Examples to refine:_
- A new movie dropped on the server is watchable on the lounge TV within N minutes,
  with correct metadata and artwork, no manual steps.
- Family members can browse and play without touching a config file.
- I can watch my library from a hotel on my phone, securely, without exposing the
  home network.

## Explicit non-goals

_What we are deliberately NOT building (at least not now). This is as important as the
goals — it bounds scope. Candidates:_
- Live TV / DVR / tuner integration
- Music-library parity with dedicated audio servers
- Photo management
- Multi-tenant / hosting for other households
- Replacing a NAS / file management

## Guiding principles / constraints

_e.g. self-hosted only; runs on commodity home hardware; security-first for anything
internet-facing; open formats; learning value._
